Zero-Shot Dysarthric Speech Reconstruction via
Inference-Time Control of Pretrained TTS Models

Abstract. Dysarthric speech severely impairs intelligibility, but existing restoration approaches typically rely on paired healthy speech, matched healthy reference speakers, or model fine-tuning, limiting their use in real-world settings. We investigate zero-shot dysarthric speech reconstruction with frozen text-to-speech models and find that F5-TTS and CosyVoice can reconstruct intelligible speech from dysarthric input alone, without requiring paired healthy data or fine tuning. We further introduce inference-time control methods, including iterative TTS generation methods and tunable activation steering, to analyze their effects on intelligibility, naturalness, and speaker similarity. Experimental results on the TORGO Database demonstrate substantial intelligibility improvements with average WER decreasing from 0.378 to 0.021 and speaker embedding similarity exceeding 0.90. Our results suggest that zero-shot TTS reconstruction combined with inference-time control offers a scalable and accessible approach to personalized dysarthric speech restoration.

Section 1

Zero-Shot Reconstruction

Given a dysarthric speaker recording (Dysarthric Prompt) and a target utterance, the model reconstructs intelligible speech in the speaker's voice without any task-specific training. For seen transcripts the prompt and target are the same utterance; for unseen transcripts the target is a held-out utterance.
