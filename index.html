Zero-Shot Dysarthric Speech Reconstruction via
Inference-Time Control of Pretrained TTS Models

Abstract. Dysarthric speech often reduces intelligibility, making dysarthric speech reconstruction an important goal for assistive speech technology. However, most existing methods rely on paired healthy speech or speaker-specific fine-tuning data, limiting their scalability. We investigate zero-shot dysarthric speech reconstruction with frozen text-to-speech models and find that F5-TTS and CosyVoice can synthesize intelligible speech without paired healthy data or fine-tuning. We further explore inference-time control methods, including iterative TTS generation and tunable activation steering, both derived from the model's own reconstructed outputs. Experimental results on the TORGO database show that iterative TTS yields the largest intelligibility gains in the regeneration setting, reducing average WER from 0.378 to 0.021. Additionally, speaker similarity remains above 0.90, naturalness increases, and utterance duration shifts toward the healthy distribution. Our findings indicate that dysarthric speech reconstruction can emerge from pretrained TTS models, and that their own reconstructed output can be reused to refine and control the result without additional training data.
